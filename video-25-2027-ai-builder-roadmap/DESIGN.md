# Video 25 Design: Cinematic Builder Depth

## Style Prompt

Video 25 uses a cinematic WebGL-inspired layered-depth system for a faceless 10-12 minute Byrddynasty episode. Real product screenshots, web-rolls, JARVIS-style database surfaces, workflow diagrams, and roadmap nodes sit in stacked spatial layers with camera pushes, parallax, glows, scanlines, masked reveals, and evidence-board motion. The look should feel like a serious builder cockpit for non-coders, business owners, and practical AI builders preparing for 2027.

## Colors

- `#071014` deep ink background
- `#0f2a31` blue-green panel depth
- `#18d6b3` JARVIS signal / active path
- `#f4c95d` roadmap checkpoints / priority highlights
- `#e8f4f2` readable foreground text
- `#b75cff` model/system accent, used sparingly

## Typography

- Primary: Inter, system UI, sans-serif
- Technical labels: SFMono-Regular, Menlo, Consolas, monospace

## Motion Rules

- Treat every screenshot or web-roll as evidence inside a spatial system, not as a flat slide.
- Use camera pushes, depth offsets, masked scroll reveals, and line-traced connections.
- Give each beat a build, reveal, comparison, transformation, or evidence moment.
- Keep critical labels still long enough to read.
- Use VO as the main audio; audio accents should be subtle and brief.

## What NOT To Do

- Do not turn the episode into generic chatbot hype.
- Do not use static centered title cards as the main visual.
- Do not expose private JARVIS content, file paths, API keys, local notes, or raw project details.
- Do not use large text blocks that compete with narration.
- Do not let public product captures become a random montage without a clear roadmap layer.
