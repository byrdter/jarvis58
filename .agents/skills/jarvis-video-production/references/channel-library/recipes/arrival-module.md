# Recipe — Arrival Module

The shape for the climax module of a part / segment. When the previous modules have been building toward THIS subject, this is the shape that says "we have arrived."

## Proven in
- Module 08 — Tools (climax of Part 1)

## Combines
- `editorial-spread` (opening eyebrow + headline-with-italic-period)
- `indicator-strip` (inherited from previous module, fades out during this one)
- `satellite-constellation` (centerpiece + 4 sub-types)
- `action-blips` (during the enumeration beat)
- `caption-ribbon` (synthesis punchline)
- `cost-meter` (foreshadow at the end for next module)

## Beat structure

```
0–4s     Open with inherited state from previous module (indicator strip with subject highlighted)
4–14s    Headline drifts in; 3–4 sibling cards in indicator strip fade out one at a time
11–22s   Subject card LIFTS from strip and grows into upper-center centerpiece
22–32s   Action blips emit from centerpiece during VO enumeration
32–60s   4 sub-type satellites appear one at a time around the centerpiece
60–72s   Synthesis caption ribbon ("FOUR ANSWERS TO ONE QUESTION")
72–end   Cost meter foreshadow appears, sets up the next module's subject
```

## Energy curve
- Opens calm (inheriting previous module's settle).
- Lifts as the strip fades and the centerpiece grows.
- Sustains medium-high through satellite reveals.
- Peaks at synthesis caption.
- Settles slightly at handoff, but cost meter keeps tension up.

## Why this shape
Climax modules need to BREAK from the previous shape. Reusing the parallel-set shape (Modules 04–07 in our case) would feel like "another sibling beat" when we want viewers to feel "this is the destination." The shape change is the narrative signal.

## When NOT to use
- Mid-arc teaching modules (use parallel-set instead).
- Part openers (use chapter-card-led opener instead).
- Closing modules (use cost-surface / chapter-card combination instead).
