Meet the cartoon starter library: a reusable kit for explainer videos that keeps production fast, consistent, and cheap.

Instead of drawing every scene from scratch, we start with two host characters, ready-made expressions, and simple poses for thinking, pointing, reacting, and presenting.

Then we add props: a laptop for research, a document for evidence, a syringe, shield, virus, atom, and microscope for science stories.

The diagrams do the heavy lifting. Arrows show cause and effect. Timelines show sequence. Bar charts compare numbers. Callout bubbles make the key idea land.

For each video, we choose a background, drop in the characters, add the right props, and animate everything with small readable moves: pop, slide, zoom, orbit, and gentle bob.

That means one new script can become a polished cartoon explainer without paying for custom video generation every time.

The test is simple: reusable assets, simple motion, one built-in voice, and a repeatable workflow.
